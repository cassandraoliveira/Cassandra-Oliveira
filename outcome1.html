1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

These days, a business without an IT component on their site can run the risk of becoming themselves obsolete. Whether it is a customer relationship management system, or a website, or an e-commerce shop, the purpose of IT in modern business serves to drive innovation, efficiency and growth within a business context. Also, the IT in a business setting can reduce costs in communication, in information processing and other areas of a business where the previous organizational structure does fail to provide an appropriate response to current business setting. For example, in these current days, where the pandemic forced a lot of businesses to adapt into new circumstances, the adherence to IT systems and solutions like e-commerce shops or deliver apps.

1.2 Describing methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully – what do you need to make projects successful? What tools? What skills?

To make a digital project successful, we need to consider no only the nature of the project but also the resources and abilities at our reach Not only we need to consider the traditional concept of project management, but also the way “digital” is conceptualized, and how these two realities collide and combine when delivering a project. As for methods, the rise of digital project made the appearance of several methods possible to follow, like Agile, Kanban, Scrum, Waterfall, amongst others - they are all part of methodology – a system of practices, techniques, procedures and rules used within a discipline (https://thedigitalprojectmanager.com/project-management-methodologies-made-simple/ ). The methods and methodology used are very important and should be used and/or adapted according to the size of the project. Regarding the methods used, they all should be easy to follow and access and must be adaptable to the size of the project.

Regarding skills, not only is necessary a good understanding of technical skills but also soft skills, like an effective communication style, that includes not only the capability of listening other opinions and other members of the team but also being able to communicate and listen to clients, in order to communicate in a clear and concise style, but that also appeals to other people.

As for tools, there a myriad of software solutions, either paid or free, that can be used to adapt. This tools can be used for either communication purposes, like Slack, for time and project management – like Trello – or for storage information, like Dropbox or Google Suit.

1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?

When projecting a digital project, the risk of failure can be quite high, specially when taking into account reasons like a undefined and unclear initial strategy and vision – like for example, a client comes over and brings an idea for a website, but does not know his brand statement, colours, target), or when final project does not fit the expectations/needs of the client, because the conceptualisation of the project did not take into account the product/service and the final costumer – the public to whom the product/service is destined to. Also, the communication and cooperation within a team may not be effective and unclear towards the members of team or the client.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

The GDPR – the EU protection data law- impacts a lot of projects not only within European context, but also worldwide. By protecting the personal data of citizens online, GDRP has put a lot of constraints in digital projects, specially of those customer-centric areas such as sales and marketing. For example, is no longer legal to hold costumer’s data without their direct consent and sharing it with other entities. However, the GDRP should be seem more that a constrains and more of legal guideline to be followed. When the law was enforced, a lot of companies panicked facing the possibility of a further digital transformation and changing of IT systems. However, this kind of guidelines can be helpful in digital projects design and management because it can give a more direct approach to how projects can be planned and new solutions can come across, more effective and innovative. Also, can be a influx of new projects just made to adjust and comply to European standards, specially from digital products/services from outside EU/EEA area.
