1.5 Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites

When taking into account copyright and other issues connected to website contents authorship, whether its image, text, video, music and such, it is important to make sure that all productions are either original or rightly referenced, with the given authorization, to avoid copyright infringement. For example, when using a image as part of content, one should pay attention not only to the source but also to the permission disclaimer, even if it is from a bank of images with free images for use.

1.6 Describing access issues that need taking into account - describe issues that people with disabilities may face when using webpages and how we can overcome them

When accessing contents on the internet, the majority of the content creators and websites fail to realize the difficulties people with disabilities have when accessing the internet. Despite the existence of applications for assisting people with visual and audible impairment access content on the internet, there is still a lot of spaces online that don’t follow guidelines like the ones by the Web Accessibility Initiative ( https://www.w3.org/WAI/standards-guidelines/wcag/), that outlines practices that help make web more inclusive and accessible to all.

For example, for people with visual impairments, it can be hard certain tasks like read text or see image properly, or even subscribe certain content due to Captcha and other anti-robot enablers. An adjustment in the coding and technical part of the project can help, like audio description of photos or products.

Another example is when content is made, it is important to see if there isn’t anything that can trigger physical reactions like seizures. More significant, is to integrate this kind of attitude into digital projects so that we don't leave a portion of potential customers and clients out.

1.7 Describe which are the appropriate filetypes for websites

For images, there is JPEG, GIF and also PNG, used in a smaller extent. This three formats can incompasse a wide rage of visual content such as pictures, gifs and vectors ( e.g. logos, digital illustrations).

As for the text, there’s PDF, the most widely know filetype created by Adobe, popular of use for documents, forms, amongst other types of written content, like online text files. It is also important to notice that PDF can include other types of visual files like JPEG.

For sound and video, there’s the option of MP3 and MP4, being MP3 connected to sound and MP4 not only sound, but also video and written content such as subtitles.
